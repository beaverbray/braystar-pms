# PRD: Market Landscape Analysis
## Property Management SaaS - Multifamily Collections Focus

**Version:** 1.0
**Status:** Research Phase
**Owner:** Market Research Team
**Last Updated:** November 2025

---

## Overview

Conduct comprehensive market landscape analysis for the U.S. property management software market, focusing on the multifamily segment (50-500 units) and collections/rent payment pain points.

## Strategic Context

- **Market Opportunity:** $1.6B (2024) → $2.5B (2030), 7.7% CAGR
- **Target Segment:** Multifamily properties, 50-500 units, professionally managed
- **Core Problem:** Late rent payments, cash flow volatility, administrative burden

## Goals & Success Metrics

### Primary Goals
1. **Validate market size and growth trajectory** with third-party data sources
2. **Identify key market drivers** and adoption trends
3. **Define actionable market segments** with sizing and prioritization
4. **Quantify pain points** with industry benchmarks and statistics

### Success Metrics
- [ ] Market size validated with ≥3 independent sources
- [ ] TAM/SAM/SOM calculations completed with supporting data
- [ ] Segment prioritization matrix created with scoring
- [ ] Pain point quantification: % of managers affected, $ impact, time impact
- [ ] Competitive landscape mapped (20+ competitors identified)

## Key Research Questions

### Market Sizing
1. What is the total addressable market (TAM) for property management software in the U.S.?
2. What is the serviceable addressable market (SAM) for multifamily 50-500 units?
3. What is the serviceable obtainable market (SOM) we can realistically capture in Year 1-3?
4. What are the growth drivers and headwinds?

### Market Segmentation
5. What are the key property management segments (SFR, multifamily, commercial, etc.)?
6. Which segments have the highest pain around collections?
7. Which segments have budget to pay for specialized solutions?
8. What are the buying behaviors and decision processes by segment?

### Technology Adoption
9. What % of property managers currently use PM software?
10. What % view technology as critical to operations?
11. What are the adoption rates for specific features (online payments, automation, AI)?
12. What are the barriers to technology adoption?

### Pain Point Quantification
13. What % of property managers cite late rent as a top operational challenge?
14. How many hours/month do managers spend on collections follow-up?
15. What is the typical on-time rent payment rate by segment?
16. What is the average DSO (days sales outstanding) for rent collection?
17. What is the typical bad debt write-off rate?

## Key Deliverables

### 1. Market Sizing Model
- **Format:** Excel/Google Sheets with formulas and sources
- **Contents:**
  - TAM calculation (total U.S. property management software market)
  - SAM calculation (multifamily 50-500 units addressable market)
  - SOM calculation (Year 1-3 realistic capture)
  - Growth projections with sensitivity analysis
  - Supporting data sources and assumptions documented

### 2. Segmentation Analysis
- **Format:** PowerPoint/Google Slides + written report
- **Contents:**
  - Market segmentation framework (property type, size, management style)
  - Segment profiles (buyer personas, pain points, budgets)
  - Prioritization matrix (pain intensity × willingness to pay × market size)
  - Beachhead segment recommendation with rationale

### 3. Pain Point Quantification Report
- **Format:** Written report with data visualizations
- **Contents:**
  - Survey data on collections pain points (if available)
  - Industry benchmark data (on-time rates, DSO, bad debt, staff time)
  - Cost of inaction analysis ($ impact of late rent)
  - Urgency and frequency scoring (how often, how critical)

### 4. Technology Adoption Analysis
- **Format:** Written report with trend analysis
- **Contents:**
  - Current adoption rates by feature category
  - Technology adoption curve positioning
  - AI/automation adoption trends in property management
  - Integration and interoperability expectations

### 5. Market Drivers & Trends Summary
- **Format:** One-page executive summary
- **Contents:**
  - Top 5 market drivers (cloud adoption, mobile, AI, consolidation, etc.)
  - Macro trends affecting property management (labor costs, regulation, etc.)
  - Technology trends (API-first, mobile-first, AI-native)
  - Risks and headwinds (regulatory, economic, competitive)

## Research Methodology

### Primary Research
- **Customer Discovery Interviews:** 20 interviews with property managers (50-500 units)
- **Industry Expert Interviews:** 5 interviews with PM consultants, association leaders
- **Survey Data:** NARPM, NAA member surveys (if available)

### Secondary Research
- **Market Research Reports:** IBISWorld, Statista, Grand View Research, MarketsandMarkets
- **Industry Reports:** NARPM, NAA, IREM annual reports and trend studies
- **Competitor Analysis:** Public filings (AppFolio 10-K), investor presentations, press releases
- **Academic Research:** Property management best practices, behavioral economics in rent collection

- NARPM State of the Industry reports

- Software review sites (G2, Capterra, Software Advice)
- Census Bureau housing data

## Risks & Mitigation

### Risk 1: Insufficient Data Availability
- **Mitigation:** Use multiple data sources, triangulate estimates, clearly document assumptions

### Risk 2: Interview Recruitment Challenges
- **Mitigation:** Leverage warm network first, offer value exchange (insights report), use LinkedIn cold outreach

### Risk 3: Market Data Contradictions
- **Mitigation:** Document all sources, use conservative estimates, sensitivity analysis on key assumptions
